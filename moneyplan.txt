  FactoryMind turns any CNC, moulder, packaging line, or oven into a machine that     
  qualifies its owner for the Piano Transizione 4.0 / 5.0 tax credit, and produces the
   live OEE dashboard + the attestazione PDF that the accountant needs to actually

  plus the operational visibility that comes free with it.

  The two problems it solves (sold separately, priced separately)                     
  
  Problem 1 — regulatory/financial: the Piano Transizione tax credit.                 
  A factory buys a machine for €120.000. If that machine is "interconnected and
  integrated into the IT system" (the three caratteristiche tecnologiche you already  
  ship), they claim:
  - Piano 4.0 base: 20% → €24.000 back                                                
  - Piano 5.0 with energy reduction ≥3–5%: up to 45% → €54.000 back                   
                                                                   
  Without your product, many SMEs either don't claim the credit, or claim it and get  
  rejected at audit because there is no telematic data trail. You are literally       
  handing them a cheque they would otherwise throw away. This is not abstract value — 
  it is €20k–€50k per machine, documented, verifiable.                                
           
  Problem 2 — operational: OEE.
  Once the telemetry is flowing, they discover their "90% utilisation" machines are
  actually running at 62% OEE. Typical SME reaction is disbelief → then anger → then  
  optimisation → then 10–15% capacity recovery without capex. This is the repeat-sale
  lever: once they trust the data, they pay for more dashboards, more lines, more     
  machines, every year.

  The "restaurant variation" — honest answer

  You mentioned different versions for manufacturer vs restaurant. For FactoryMind    
  specifically: no, don't fork it for restaurants. FactoryMind's load-bearing value is
   the Piano 4.0/5.0 attestazione, which restaurants cannot claim. Forcing it into    
  hospitality would mean stripping the one thing that makes it sell.
         
  But the underlying stack (MQTT + time-series + dashboards + alerts) is absolutely   
  reusable. For a restaurant the equivalent product is:
  - HACCP temperature compliance monitoring (fridges, freezers, cooking core)         
  - Energy/gas consumption per service                                                
  - POS-integrated covers-vs-food-cost live P&L
                                                                                      
  That is a different product built on the same skeleton. Keep it in mind but don't   
  split your focus yet. Ship FactoryMind to one real manufacturer first. Everything   
  else is a distraction until that happens.                                           
                                                                                      
  Packaging model that fits "keep the core, sell the skin"                            
         
  Three tiers. Same codebase. Different wrapping.                                     
           
  Tier 1 — FactoryMind Community (free, self-hosted).                                 
  The current open MIT repo. Lets you claim "X factories running it" on your site.
  Lets accountants try it in their lab. This is your billboard, not your revenue.     
           
  Tier 2 — FactoryMind Standard (per-facility licence + install).                     
  Your installed product. One-time setup fee (€2.500–€5.000), then annual support
  subscription (€1.200–€3.000/year per facility). Includes: installation, custom      
  machine/line mapping, one attestazione per year, email support, minor
  customisations. Deploy on their hardware or a small VPS. This is your bread and     
  butter.  
                                                                                      
  Tier 3 — FactoryMind Enterprise / "Bollato" (custom fork, their branding).          
  For clients who want it branded as theirs or with proprietary extensions.
  €10.000–€25.000 one-time + annual. Custom PDF layouts with their logo, integration  
  with their ERP, dedicated SLA. The base codebase stays yours; the fork is a
  work-for-hire deliverable. Critical: contract specifies you retain the base, they   
  get their customisations + a perpetual licence to use. Italian contract term:
  "licenza d'uso non esclusiva + prestazione personalizzata".

  Tier 4 — FactoryMind Cloud (your SaaS). Later, once 3–5 Tier 2 deployments are      
  stable. Monthly subscription, you host, €150–€400/machine/month. Don't start here —
  infrastructure cost is real and the local SME buyer is allergic to monthly          
  subscriptions with their data in someone else's cloud.

  How to price without a portfolio yet

  First customer: half price, on the record. Ask in return for a written testimonial +
   permission to visit with future prospects. That's your portfolio. Within 6 months
  of the first install you should raise prices to the numbers above.                  
           
  Never do free. Free customers are the worst customers. Charge a symbolic €500       
  minimum if you must discount aggressively. Paid = respected.
                                                                                      
  Trust without credentials — the 5 moves                                             
  
  1. Partita IVA with proper SDI — you already live in the Italian tax system, make it
   visible. Have your own FatturaPA workflow sorted (ironically, your FatturaFlow
  project).                                                                           
  2. Public GitHub repo with working demo. A commercialista can't evaluate Rust, but
  can see "active, maintained, real code" and pass that to their IT nephew.           
  3. Italian landing page with a 3-minute video of the live dashboard + the
  attestazione PDF coming out. Not slick — honest, shot at your desk, in Italian,     
  ending with "chiamami al +39…". This is the single highest-converting asset you can
  make.                                                                               
  4. Local presence. One talk at Confartigianato Verona or Confindustria Verona
  sezione meccanica. One coffee with the president of the Consorzio del Mobile. These 
  cost nothing and put you on a printed list.
  5. The attestazione itself. Deliver one signed, PDF-rendered, cross-referenced to   
  Circolari. An accountant who sees your attestazione lands in their inbox and matches
   their checklist will remember you for life.
                                                                                      
  What you need to ship to make FactoryMind sellable this quarter                     
  
  The code is in PASS state per the Mission II audit. What's missing is the commercial
   packaging layer:
                                                                                      
  1. Piano 4.0/5.0 attestazione as a real PDF — currently a pure-function data model. 
  Needs a PDF renderer (puppeteer or pdfkit) that produces a signed document with the
  client's data, machine serials, interconnection proof, and the exact Circolare      
  citations. This is your killer deliverable.
  2. Onboarding script — a one-command installer that pulls compose, asks 5 questions
  (facility name, line count, machine list), writes .env, starts services, opens the  
  dashboard. No manual Docker Compose yet for a non-technical client.
  3. Machine connector library — currently OPC UA, Modbus, Sparkplug. Need 2–3        
  pre-built adapters for the machines your first 3 prospects actually own. Go visit   
  them and ask. Common ones in your area: Fanuc FOCAS, Siemens S7 via Snap7,
  Heidenhain DNC, generic Modbus RTU over RS-485.                                     
  4. Commercial collateral — the landing page exists but is placeholder-grade. Needs:
  value proposition in 10 seconds, the €24k/machine number front and centre, a contact
   form, a downloadable sample attestazione PDF, Italian testimonials (even if the
  first one is "pilot customer at facility X, reduced downtime 12%").                 
  5. Legal template — simple Italian contract for Tier 2: "licenza + installazione +
  canone annuale di manutenzione", liability cap, data ownership clause (their data is
   theirs, stays on their hardware), exit clause. Two pages. A lawyer writes this for
  €300.                                                                               
  6. Your own pilot — install FactoryMind on a Raspberry Pi at a friend's workshop,
  even a non-paying friend. Run it for 30 days. Screenshot everything. That is your   
  first reference.
